### Rokis/Seoul Mainnet Parameters

Rokis is scheduled as a future hard fork on the Seoul network. At the fork
boundary, VCT does not reset or replace the existing ECCPoW difficulty. The
first Rokis block inherits the parent difficulty from the Seoul chain, and the
VRF sortition threshold starts fully open so that VCT does not introduce an
additional liveness bottleneck at the transition.

```text
network: Seoul
chainId: 103
VCTBlock: 10,000,000
pre-VCT phase: block 0..9,999,999, Seoul/ECCPoW compatible
VCT phase: block 10,000,000+, Rokis/WIP-6
S0: 100 WL

fork difficulty policy: inherit parent difficulty
initial sortitionThreshold: 2^256 (p = 1.0, bootstrap all eligible)
P_base (target SortitionBase): 2^253 (12.5%, p* = 1/8)
TimeoutStart: 15 seconds
TimeoutEnd: 60 seconds
future timestamp tolerance F: 15 seconds
```

The Seoul chain currently runs at a non-trivial ECCPoW difficulty. Therefore
Rokis mainnet activation should preserve the parent difficulty rather than
resetting it to a testnet floor. The bootstrap controller first lowers
`sortitionThreshold` from `2^256` toward `P_base`; only after reaching the target
threshold does ECCPoW difficulty resume ordinary adjustment. This preserves
pre-fork mining conditions while allowing the VRF gate to become selective
gradually.

The corresponding chain configuration is:

```go
VCTBlock: big.NewInt(10_000_000)
Vct: &VctConfig{
    MinEligibleBalance:       100 WL,
    InitialSortitionThreshold: 256,
}
```
